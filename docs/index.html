01 · Overview

End-to-End Alpha Research Workflow

From raw price data to a validated long-short strategy with rigorous OOS testing and realistic cost modeling.

This project constructs a complete equity alpha signal pipeline across 12 large-cap tickers. Historical price and benchmark data are transformed into a rich feature set then fed into Random Forest, Gradient Boosting, and LSTM models.

Signal quality is evaluated with IC and ICIR across rolling folds. Portfolio performance is assessed with Sharpe, drawdown, turnover, and Calmar — all net of realistic transaction costs.

The final model pred_final_ens_lstm achieves net Sharpe 0.6369 and net return 25.91% with max drawdown of only 15.42%.

06 · Research Findings

Key Takeaways

🏆
Final Model SelectionEnsemble + LSTM was selected as the final clean model, balancing IC quality with strong net Sharpe and manageable drawdown.
🔀
LSTM as Blend SignalLSTM added temporal patterns but performed best as a minority blend rather than a standalone predictor.
📊
IC ≠ SharpeSignals with the highest Mean IC did not necessarily produce the highest net Sharpe — full portfolio-level evaluation is essential.
💸
Transaction Costs MatterIncluding realistic costs materially changed model rankings and interpretation of signal value.
🔬
Sector Neutralization as DiagnosticApplied as a diagnostic only — the 12-ticker universe is too small for reliable sector-neutral construction.
📉
Fold 2 UnderperformanceFold 2 showed regime-specific underperformance attributable to market conditions, not a feature engineering problem.
07 · Limitations

Scope and Constraints

Important limitations apply before drawing generalizable conclusions.

✗
Small UniverseOnly 12 large-cap tickers — insufficient for robust cross-sectional generalization
✗
Simplified Cost ModelFixed estimates, not adaptive market-impact models
✗
Equal-Weight ConstructionLong-short uses equal weights, not optimized position sizing
✗
No Factor Risk ModelNo multi-factor risk model for exposure control or hedging
✗
Regime SensitivityLSTM performance is regime-dependent and may not generalize
✗
Not a Live SystemResearch and backtesting framework only — not production trading
